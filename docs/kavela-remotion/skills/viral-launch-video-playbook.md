# Viral Launch Video Playbook

## The 3.5M View Formula: AIDA Framework

Every launch video MUST follow this structure. No exceptions.

### A — ATTENTION (0:00–0:05)
**Goal:** Stop the scroll in under 1 second.
- Open with the PROBLEM, not your product
- Use a shocking stat or universally relatable pain
- Show real-world chaos (actual app UIs, scattered windows, messy workflows)
- The viewer must think "that's me" within 3 seconds

### I — INTEREST (0:05–0:12)
**Goal:** Build empathy, then introduce the solution.
- Validate the viewer: "You're not [bad thing]. You're [real problem]."
- Emotional pivot from problem to possibility
- Logo reveal with breathing space — DON'T rush it
- One-line tagline that is poetic, not corporate. Setup line + payoff line.

### D — DESIRE (0:12–0:25)
**Goal:** Show USE CASES, not features. Outcomes, not capabilities.
- Show the product IN a real workflow, not in isolation
- Each use case = an OUTCOME pill: "Done! Your email reply is ready."
- Show HOW to trigger it (one simple action)
- Demonstrate on a real desktop/workflow — ground it in reality
- Multiple use cases shown as rapid-fire but READABLE text

### A — ACTION (0:25–0:30)
**Goal:** CTA with an incentive.
- Platform/ecosystem proof: "Works everywhere you do" with orbiting icons
- Social proof metric: "2x faster", "10,000 teams"
- Simple CTA: URL + tagline
- End on logo — bookends with the open

## Lemon.ai Technique Extraction (3.5M Views)

### 1. Typography as the Hero
- **Bold serif font** (Playfair Display style) — NOT sans-serif. Editorial, NYT-headline energy. Premium feel.
- **Massive font size** — text fills 70%+ of screen width. ONE idea per screen.
- **Word-by-word staggered reveal** — key stat words appear at different times with different sizes. "1,100 times" is HUGE, "per day" is slightly smaller.
- **Italic for emphasis** — "into what gets *done*" — the payoff word is italicized.

### 2. The Hand-Drawn Accent
- A **yellow hand-drawn underline** under the word "interrupted" — organic, imperfect, human.
- This one detail breaks the clean minimalism and adds warmth.
- **Kavela equivalent**: Use a colored brush-stroke underline or highlight under key emotional words. Can be SVG path animated.

### 3. Stats as Hooks
- "The average knowledge worker switches tabs 1,100 times per day"
- "spends 3 hours typing"
- These are **relatable, shocking, and specific**. Not vague claims.
- **Kavela equivalent**: "30% of AI-assisted time is spent correcting output" or "5 devs, 5 different .cursorrules, 5 different AI outputs"

### 4. Real App Screenshots as Visual Chaos
- Slack messages, Google Docs typing, Meeting Notes, Gmail — REAL recognizable UI floating and overlapping.
- NOT abstract shapes or mockups. The viewer instantly recognizes their daily tools.
- **Kavela equivalent**: Show .cursorrules files, CLAUDE.md, Cursor settings, scattered README.md — actual file icons people recognize.

### 5. Voice Waveform / Audio Visualization
- Rounded pills with barcode-like sound waveforms floating around the tagline.
- Represents the product's core mechanic (voice-first) as a VISUAL METAPHOR.
- **Kavela equivalent**: Brain neural network visualization, context flow lines connecting nodes, or skill-card particles flowing into AI tools.

### 6. 3D Physical Object Illustration
- A realistic macOS fn key rendered as a 3D keyboard key with shadow and brand-color accent.
- Makes the abstract ("press a button") feel PHYSICAL and tangible.
- **Kavela equivalent**: A 3D rendered MCP plug/connector, or a stylized keyboard shortcut visualization.

### 7. Outcome Action Pills
- Dark rounded rectangles with logo icon: "Done! Your email reply is ready for review", "Fixed: I've rewritten your Slack message"
- Shows what the product DOES as completed notifications, not feature descriptions.
- These cascade in from different angles with stagger.
- **Kavela equivalent**: "✓ check_context — 3 skills found", "✓ UI Design Principles — 87.5% match", "✓ Standards applied automatically"

### 8. Desktop-as-Canvas
- Full macOS desktop shown with real content (article) and product overlay.
- Grounds the demo in the viewer's actual environment.
- The macOS wallpaper, dock, and menu bar are all visible.
- **Kavela equivalent**: Show a real Cursor/VS Code window with Claude Code terminal, Kavela overlay appearing naturally.

### 9. Platform Orbit
- App icons arranged in a loose, slightly tilted orbit around center text.
- NOT a rigid grid — each icon is rotated slightly, organic placement.
- Shows ecosystem breadth without listing features.
- **Kavela equivalent**: Claude, Cursor, VS Code, Windsurf, ChatGPT icons orbiting "One brain. Every AI tool."

### 10. Color Restraint
- Maximum 2-3 colors on screen at any time.
- Black text, white background, brand yellow as ONLY accent.
- The accent color (yellow) is used SPARINGLY — underlines, key highlights, keyboard accents.
- No gradients. No complex backgrounds. Typography does the heavy lifting.
- **Kavela equivalent**: White bg (#ffffff), near-black text (#0f172a), brand blue (#3b82f6) as only accent.

### 11. Breathing Space
- Logo icon gets its own FULL SCREEN with nothing else.
- Single words ("Meet") get their own full screen.
- Generous whitespace — never cramped.
- The pause between sections is what makes each hit land harder.

### 12. Empathy Before Solution
- "You're not unproductive" — VALIDATES the viewer first.
- THEN "You're interrupted" — reframes the problem.
- THEN the solution. Never lead with the solution.
- **Kavela equivalent**: "Your AI isn't broken." → "It's blind." → "Kavela."

### 13. Bookend Logo
- Opens with logo. Closes with logo. Clean symmetry.
- The logo is ALWAYS the simple icon, never text-heavy.

### 14. Staggered Element Entrance
- Nothing appears all at once. Each element enters 100-200ms after the previous one.
- Creates a cascading waterfall reading flow.
- Even within a single stat, words stagger: "switches" (beat) "tabs" (beat) "1,100 times" (SLAM) "per day" (settle).

## Kavela-Adapted AIDA Storyboard

### ATTENTION — "Your AI doesn't know your team's rules"
1. Open: icon.png logo snap (0.5s)
2. Scattered real files float in: `.cursorrules`, `CLAUDE.md`, `.windsurfrules`, `copilot.yml`, `.claude/settings`, `rules.md`, `.ai-config`
3. Stat: "5 devs. 5 context files. 5 different AI outputs."
4. Files remain drifting — visual chaos
5. "Your AI isn't broken." → "It's blind." (empathy slam with zoom out)

### INTEREST — "Meet Kavela"
1. White flash → icon.png centered, breathing
2. "Kavela" mask wipe
3. Tagline word-by-word: "One brain. Every AI tool."

### DESIRE — Show Use Cases as Outcomes
1. **Define once.** — Terminal card showing `create_skill` with skill content (collaborative context writing)
2. **Apply everywhere.** — User asks "Design me a dashboard" → check_context runs (animated looping dots) → outcome pills cascade: "check_context ✓", "87.5% match", "get_skill ✓", "Standards applied ✓"
3. **Works in every AI tool.** — Claude, Cursor, VS Code, Windsurf, ChatGPT icons orbit around center text. Spring-based rotation.

### ACTION — CTA with incentive
1. Rapid outcome statements (2s each, uniform timing):
   - "New Hire, Full Context"
   - "Same standards. Every tool."
   - "Stop re-entering context."
   - "Start compounding it."
2. Logo spring + "kavela.ai" + "One brain. Every AI tool." + "Free early access signups starts now"
3. Hold 3.5s+ on final CTA screen

## Production Rules

1. **Never show features. Show outcomes.** "check_context" is an outcome. "Skill matching" is a feature.
2. **One idea per screen.** If you need a second sentence, you need a second screen.
3. **Beat-sync everything.** Every text appearance, every transition, every element entrance lands on a beat or half-beat.
4. **Serif for emotional text, sans-serif for UI.** Georgia for slam statements. Mono for CLI/code.
5. **Accent ONE key word per sentence.** Blue color on "zero", "different", "blind" — never more than one.
6. **Stats must be specific.** "30% of time correcting output" not "lots of time wasted".
7. **Empathy before pitch.** Always validate the viewer's frustration before showing the solution.
8. **White flash as scene separator.** A 2-3 frame white flash between major sections creates a cinematic cut feel.
9. **Logo bookends.** Open on logo, close on logo.
10. **Test on Twitter with sound OFF.** If the video doesn't work on mute, it fails. Every message must be readable as text.
11. **Text always needs more reading time than you think.** When in doubt, add 0.5s dead end.
12. **CTA copy: kavela.ai** (not .dev). Incentive: "Free early access signups starts now".
